# Shardwake — Combat

This document defines the combat philosophy and gameplay rules.

Combat should feel tactical, readable and rewarding.

The goal is not fast kills.

The goal is meaningful decisions.

---

# Core Combat

Players fight using:

- auto attacks
- active skills
- dodge roll
- weapon swapping
- positioning

Combat is designed around timing rather than button mashing.

---

# Auto Attack

Auto attack targets the nearest valid enemy.

Auto attacks exist to reduce mobile controls.

Auto attacks should never be the strongest source of damage.

Their purpose is to:

- keep combat flowing
- finish enemies
- apply weapon pressure

The player still wins fights through movement, positioning and skill usage.

---

# Dodge Roll

Every player has dodge roll.

Roll is the primary defensive mechanic.

Roll consumes Energy.

Roll can avoid:

- melee attacks
- projectiles
- skill shots
- boss attacks

Armor weight affects roll performance.

---

# Weapon Skills

Every weapon contains:

- 6 active skills
- player equips 3
- 2 passive skills
- player equips 1

The player equips two weapons.

This creates:

- 6 active skills total
- 2 passive skills

Weapon swap should be fast enough to encourage hybrid builds.

---

# Combat Pace

Combat should be slower than a MOBA.

Players should have enough time to:

- react
- dodge
- reposition
- punish mistakes

The average duel should last significantly longer than a typical battle royale fight.

---

# Enemy Design

Every enemy should have readable attacks.

Enemies should telegraph dangerous abilities.

Different enemies should create different combat problems.

Examples:

- charger
- caster
- healer
- summoner
- tank
- assassin

Enemy variety is more important than enemy health.

---

# Boss Design

Bosses should rely on mechanics, not massive HP.

Boss fights should reward:

- observation
- positioning
- timing

rather than raw DPS.

---

# PvP

PvP should reward:

- positioning
- cooldown management
- weapon combinations
- good movement

not simply pressing every skill immediately.

---

# Main Rule

Combat should never feel automatic.

Auto attacks support combat.

Player decisions win combat.
