# Shardwake Art and Product Direction

## Visual target

Shardwake should feel like a bright, stylized mobile fantasy extraction RPG.

The gameplay inspiration is Dark and Darker, but the presentation should be closer to a polished mobile action RPG:

- colorful top-down fantasy
- chunky readable silhouettes
- friendly but dangerous monsters
- clear rarity colors
- punchy skill icons
- strong UI readability on phones
- cartoon/stylized, not grim realistic horror

## Reference interpretation

The provided concept board points toward:

- top-down forest/dungeon arenas with paths, ruins, portals, monsters, and chests
- class lineup: Warrior, Rogue, Ranger, Mage, Priest, Necromancer
- large mobile HUD with joystick, minimap, HP/MP, skill buttons, consumables, and quick action slots
- inventory/equipment screen with rarity-framed item slots
- multiple portal colors representing difficulty or destination risk
- Haven as a compact town hub with tavern, shop, quest board, and storage
- biome/map tiles: forest, desert, snow, swamp/ruins, lava
- enemies with strong silhouettes: skeletons, goblins, treants, boars/brutes, spirits
- map identity comes from readable biome modules around a recognizable dangerous center landmark

## Product positioning

Shardwake is not trying to copy Dark and Darker visually.

The intended position is:

> Dark and Darker extraction tension, adapted into a readable, colorful, mobile top-down action RPG.

## UI direction

The mobile combat HUD should support:

- joystick on the left
- autoattack
- dash/dodge button
- three skill buttons
- two consumable slots
- minimap
- portal timer/status
- quick loot/session status

The meta UI should support:

- class/loadout selection
- equipment slots
- carried loot vs equipped gear
- stash/storage
- rarity legend
- portal/difficulty selection
- Haven buildings

## Immediate prototype priorities

1. Replace open arena with rooms, corridors, walls, and guarded chests.
2. Add enemy telegraphs so dash/dodge matters.
3. Scatter multiple extraction portals around the map.
4. Add a simple inventory/equipment panel that visually separates equipped gear from carried loot.
5. Add an AI rival Seeker placeholder before real network multiplayer.
6. Promote biome modules from greybox colors into authored forest, cemetery, swamp, lava, snow, and road tilesets.

## Guardrails

- Do not make the game visually grim, muddy, or realistic.
- Do not overcomplicate controls before combat feels good.
- Do not build large meta-progression before the extraction loop is fun.
- Keep AI as flavor only, never gameplay authority.
- Keep online rules shaped for server authority even while prototyping locally.
