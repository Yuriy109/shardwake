# Shardwake — Design Philosophy

Shardwake is built around tension, choice, and memorable runs.

The player should never feel that the game plays itself.

The player should constantly make decisions:
- fight or avoid
- loot or extract
- enter Hellgate or leave
- wear heavy armor or stay mobile
- use safe gear or risk valuable gear

## Core Emotion

The main emotion is:

"I found something valuable. Can I get out alive?"

## Main Rules

### 1. Greed is dangerous

The longer a player stays, the more they can gain, but the more they can lose.

### 2. Every build has a weakness

No weapon, armor setup, or stat combination should solve everything.

### 3. Death must feel fair

When the player dies, they should understand why.

Bad death:
"I had no chance."

Good death:
"I got greedy."
"I wasted my roll."
"I ignored the caster."
"I entered Hellgate too late."

### 4. Mobile readability first

Every enemy attack, skill, portal, loot item, and danger zone must be readable on a phone screen.

### 5. AI adds memory, not power

AI makes runs and items feel personal.

AI never gives stats, rewards, or gameplay advantage.

### 6. The world should remember the player

Successful extractions create Chronicles.

Important items can gain stories.

The player is not just farming loot — they are creating legends.

## What Makes Shardwake Different

Shardwake is not about being the last survivor.

It is about choosing how much risk you can survive.

The best moment in the game should be:

"I should leave now... but maybe one more room."
