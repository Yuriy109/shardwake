# Shardwake — Loot System

Loot is the main reward for taking risks.

The goal is not to collect the most items.

The goal is to extract with the most valuable items.

---

# Loot Sources

Loot can be obtained from:

- monsters
- mini bosses
- Hellgate bosses
- chests
- elite enemies
- world events
- hidden rooms

Higher risk always increases potential rewards.

---

# Equipment Rarity

Current rarity tiers:

- Common (White)
- Uncommon (Green)
- Rare (Blue)
- Epic (Purple)
- Legendary (Orange)

Future tiers may be added later.

---

# Equipment Progression

## Common

- Base armor or weapon stats only
- No bonus attributes

Starter equipment.

---

## Uncommon

- 2 random stat points
- Distributed between one or more attributes

---

## Rare

- 3 random stat points
- One attribute is guaranteed to receive +2
- Better value ranges

---

## Epic

- 4 random stat points
- One attribute is guaranteed to receive +2
- Higher stat ranges
- Higher chance for special affixes

---

## Legendary

- Highest stat values
- Multiple powerful affixes
- Unique passive effect
- AI-generated name and lore

Legendary items should feel memorable.

---

# Affixes

Equipment may roll:

- Strength
- Dexterity
- Intelligence
- Agility
- Vitality
- Focus
- Stamina

Future unique affixes may also exist.

---

# Backpack

Players have limited inventory space.

Large items consume more slots.

Choosing what to keep is part of the gameplay.

Inventory management should create meaningful decisions.

---

# Death

On death:

- equipped items drop
- backpack items drop
- other players may loot everything

Starter gear can always be reclaimed.

---

# Insurance

Players may insure up to two equipped items before entering a run.

If another player extracts with the insured item:

- it is permanently lost.

If nobody extracts with it:

- it returns to the owner after the match.

Insurance should reduce frustration without removing risk.

---

# Design Goals

Loot should create excitement.

Finding a valuable item should immediately create tension.

The player should constantly ask:

"Should I keep exploring...

...or should I extract now?"


# Shardwake — Loot, Death & Extraction

This document defines the loot, extraction, death and inventory systems.

These systems create the core tension of Shardwake.

---

# Core Principle

Loot has value only if the player successfully extracts.

Greed should always be tempting.

Death should always matter.

---

# Death

If the player dies:

- equipped items drop
- backpack items drop
- relics drop
- gold and crafting materials drop

Other players may loot everything.

Starter gear can always be reclaimed in Haven.

---

# Starter Gear

Before every run the player may equip:

- Weapon 1
- Weapon 2
- Light / Medium / Heavy starter armor

Starter gear is:

- Common quality
- Cannot be traded
- Has no affixes
- Always reclaimable

Starter gear exists so players are never locked out of the game.

---

# Insurance

Players may insure up to **2 equipped items** before entering a run.

Rules:

- only equipped items can be insured
- backpack items cannot be insured
- insured items still drop on death
- other players can loot insured items
- if another player extracts with an insured item, it is permanently lost
- if nobody extracts with it, the item returns after the match

Insurance reduces frustration but never removes risk.

---

# Extraction

Solo mode:

- 16 players
- 8 extraction portals
- one portal extracts one player
- portal disappears after use

Extraction requires channeling.

Suggested MVP:

- 3 second interaction
- taking damage cancels extraction

---

# Portal Flow

0:00 – Match begins

6:00 – First portals activate

8:30 – Hellgate entrances appear

9:00 – More portals activate

11:00 – Final portals activate

12:00 – Normal Shard collapses

---

# Hellgate

Hellgate is optional overtime.

Players choose:

- extract safely
- or risk Hellgate

Hellgate:

- begins after the normal match ends
- lasts up to 3 minutes
- stronger enemies
- better loot
- fewer extraction portals
- much higher PvP pressure

Not everyone should survive Hellgate.

# Secondary Affixes

Examples:

- Armor
- Magic Resistance
- Critical Chance
- Critical Damage
- Attack Speed
- Movement Speed
- Energy Regeneration
- Healing Power
- Summon Power
- Fire Damage
- Frost Damage
- Poison Damage
- Shadow Damage
- Holy Damage
- Luck

Luck is **not** a core stat.

Luck may:

- slightly improve loot quality
- increase bonus loot roll chance
- improve material drops

Luck must never become mandatory.

---

# Loot Design Rules

The best loot should require the greatest risk.

Players should constantly ask:

> "Should I leave now...
> or risk one more room?"

Loot is successful only when it reaches Haven.
